Our Projects

Supporting communities through sustainable initiatives around the world.

Batwa Farms at Matanda, Uganda

Goal One: Sustainability

The Omotani Caring Foundation has purchased approximately 10 acres of very fertile and productive farmland at Matanda (near Kihihi) Uganda. In collaboration with the Rafiki Memorial Wildlife Conservation Initiative, this farming project involves and supports Batwa families from the Kibirangwe, Nyabisiika, Mukongoro and Karehe settlements.

Leaders, elders, youth, and other family representatives from each settlement have visited the farm land and have voluntarily agreed to be participants in this project.

Project Leadership: Tweheyo Robert and Mushamba Moses provide the training and support leadership for this farm project.

Porters at Bwindi

Goal Two: Sustainability

Supporting the development and expansion of food-related sustainability projects that allow porters in porter associations to supplement the income they receive from tourism.

The OCF provides small annual grants to purchase bees for honey and chickens for eggs. Porters have already established 35 bee hives that are producing honey.

This project started with the Bwindi Bujengwe Porters Association and has been coordinated with the support and project accountability controls of the Rafiki Wildlife Conservation Initiative.

Learn More at Rafiki Wildlife

The Partners Nepal

Goal Eight: Strengthen Relationships

Drs. Les and Barbara Omotani met Ang Rita Sherpa and Peter Hillary during a 2014 National Geographic Expedition. Throughout the years, they have provided private donations to support the work of THE PARTNERS NEPAL (TPN).

The Partners Nepal is a non-profit, Non-Government Organization (NGO) registered in Solukhumbu, Province No 1 in Nepal. TPN has a solid record of good financial management of donor funds and quality planning and completion of projects.

The OCF serves as a key communicator and link to The Partners Nepal, helping US citizens find an easy and legal way to donate funds and qualify for federal tax credits.

Women in Gorilla Conservation

Goal Nine: Conservation

The Omotani Caring Foundation collaborates with Sahaya International and the Rafiki Memorial Wildlife Conservation Initiative to support a project initiated by Robinah Gangiriba (Warden of Tourism of the Ugandan Wildlife Authority).

This important and emerging project was recommended by our senior advisor Paul Kirui. The OCF has made an initial donation to support the purchasing of a new sewing machine, fabrics and other materials.

Base Camp for Veterans

Goal Ten: Veteran Support

BaseCamp for Veterans, Inc. (BCI) was founded by life-long friends Deborah Plum & Theo Windish. Their mission is to respond to the needs of those who serve our nation and protect its values, by promoting personal and professional growth, and contributing to an effective, sustainable path towards reintegration.

The OCF has provided support to BCI for 2024, 2025 and beyond. In particular, we have supported the work of the mustang trainer, Randy Helm, and his work with introducing veterans to the unique connection available with mustangs.

Visit BCI Website

Randy Helm Horsemanship

Goal Eleven: Equine Programs

We support Randy Helm and his continuing work to train wild mustangs and support a wide variety of people to learn good and great horsemanship skills.

Randy has successfully led the State of Arizona's Wild Horse Inmate program and is a facilitator with Base Camp for Veterans. He leads amazing horsemanship and riding clinics throughout the United States.

We are working to help wild mustangs find forever homes and be transitioned from the wild into amazing trained horses for riders.

High Asia Habitat Fund

Goal Twelve: Conservation

The Omotani Caring Foundation supports the High Asia Habitat Fund (HAHF). The founder, Behzad Larry, is committed to the conservation of snow leopards and other wildlife in the region.

HAHF is also supporting the training of local people to help them and their cattle coexist with wildlife in the remote regions of the high Himalayan mountains.

Current study and exploration is being conducted to determine whether brown bears can become a valued natural resource for future tourism and conservation.
